The Market Problem

Three problems enterprise APM
tools were never built to solve

Europe has 230+ GW of installed wind capacity. 40–60 GW is exiting OEM warranty right now. The enterprise solutions built for large, single-OEM, in-warranty fleets structurally cannot serve this market.

Pain 01

The Post-Warranty Crisis

Thousands of European turbines are simultaneously exiting OEM monitoring contracts in 2025–2026. Standard RM&D service agreements run 5–10 years — and that clock started between 2010 and 2016.

  • OEM monitoring renewals cost €80–120/turbine/month and escalate significantly post-warranty
  • The same OEM quoting your renewal is quoting you a new turbine
  • Operators must choose: pay the OEM premium, or fly blind
40 GW+ exiting OEM warranty across Europe by 2027
Pain 02

Mixed-Fleet Blindness

An IPP that has grown through acquisition operates Vestas, GE, and Enercon turbines — and runs three separate monitoring portals. No OEM will give a competitor's turbines visibility in their platform.

  • Three monitoring portals, three alert systems, three maintenance workflows
  • No unified fleet health view across turbine manufacturers
  • No cross-turbine risk ranking — no single source of truth
60% of mid-tier IPPs operate turbines from 2 or more OEMs
Pain 03

Prediction Without Prioritisation

Most PdM tools answer one question: "Is something wrong with this turbine?" Almost none answer the operational question maintenance engineers actually need answered.

  • "Given 14 active alerts across my 60-turbine fleet — what do I fix first?"
  • "With which crew, in what sequence, before Thursday's weather window?"
  • Alert volume without rank order is noise, not intelligence
Risk-ranked prioritisation reduces crew mobilisation cost by 20–35%

Core Capabilities

Engineering-grade intelligence.

Every alert in WindSense AI is traceable to a failure mode, a sensor signature, and a consequence class. Engineers can defend every recommendation to management, auditors, and technical reviewers.

Fault Prediction Engine

Failure Mode Classification

Classifies alerts to specific component failure modes — gearbox bearing wear, generator winding degradation, hydraulic seal failure, transformer thermal overload — not generic anomalies.

FMECA · Failure Mode Library

Remaining Useful Life (RUL)

Per-component RUL estimation for gearbox, main bearing, generator, and pitch system. Gives operators a planning horizon — "38 days before this component requires intervention."

Prognostics · Planning

FMECA Risk Ranking

Dynamic Risk Priority Number (RPN) = Probability × Consequence × Detectability. Ranks every active alert across the fleet so your engineer knows what to action before the next weather window.

Risk Matrix · RPN Queue
